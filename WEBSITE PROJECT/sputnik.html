    • Sputnik V is an adenovirus{group of common virus which causes normal fever} viral vector vaccine for COVID-19 developed by the Gamaleya Research Institute of Epidemiology and Microbiology in Russia. It is the world's first registered combination vector vaccine for the prevention of COVID-19, having been registered on 11 August 2020 by the Russian Ministry of Health.The 'V' in its name refers to 'Victory [over COVID-19]', according to Kirill Dmitriev, CEO of the Russian Direct Investment Fund. Its 90% affective against both CoronaVirus and Delta Variant. The first dose is administered on the first day, and the second dose is administered on the 21st day to boost immune response. Both doses are administered into the deltoid muscle{It forms the round contour of our shoulder}. Doesn't have any side effect. It is only applicable for age group above 18.

    Manufacturing of the Vaccine

    Large quantities of both adenoviruses are produced by HEK 293 cells{these are immortalized human embryonic kidney cells.} that have the E1 gene {protiens} necessary for viral replication. Rarely, Ad5{adenovirus} can acquire the E1 gene from the HEK 293 cells, restoring its ability to replicate. SputnikV has set an acceptable limit of 5,000 replicating virus particles per vaccine dose, and quality control documents state that tested batches contain less than 100 replicating virus particles per dose.
